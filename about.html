About Tensorgami

Make the mechanism visible.

Mathematical Writing

Purpose

Tensorgami is a public mathematical workbench by Henry Shin. Its mandate is to make mathematical mechanisms explicit: to take results that may be known, standard, or abstractly packaged, and rebuild them in concrete, algorithmic, inspectable form.

The archive includes expository reconstructions, proof notes, a problem workbench, and formalized fragments. Recurring tools include explicit reductions and normal forms, quantitative and finitary structure, concrete decompositions, and selective formal verification when it clarifies the argument.

Henry studied mathematics at Harvard, where he worked with Richard Taylor.

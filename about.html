About Tensorgami

Mathematical Writing

Purpose

Tensorgami is a public archive of mathematical writing maintained by Henry Shin. It collects expositions, proof notes, problem sets, and Lean-checked formalizations, with an emphasis on arguments that are readable, inspectable, and worth preserving.

Henry studied mathematics under Richard Taylor while Taylor was at Harvard; his name appears on Taylor's former-students page, now hosted at Stanford. After a period in industry, he returned to independent mathematical writing and formalization. This site is a place to share mathematics that is deep, useful, or simply beautiful.

Some proof-focused entries include Lean source files and automated checks, so readers can compare the written exposition with a formally verified version of the argument.

Mandate

Explicit. Algorithmic. Concrete.

Tensorgami is a mathematical workbench for making abstract results explicit, algorithmic, concrete, and inspectable.

The archive is broad by subject but narrow by method. Across algebra, analysis, number theory, topology, combinatorics, and formalization, the recurring aim is to take a theorem apart until the working mechanism is visible: the coordinate system, the reduction, the obstruction, the bookkeeping, the algorithm, or the exact point where rigidity enters.

PDF expositions and proof notes organized by subject, but shaped by a common method: exposing the proof mechanism beneath the usual packaging. Lean source and automated checks are included when formal verification materially supports the note.

How to read this archive

The notes are not meant only as a subject-area syllabus. They are a mechanism library. Many entries revisit standard results in order to expose the proof engine beneath the usual packaging.

Notes are expository unless explicitly marked otherwise; Lean-checked entries link both source and CI.
